December 25 is a time of joy and celebration. It was the birthdate of one of the greatest men who ever lived. Even though his real father wasn't physically present during his birth, he was still there in spirit.

This year, during the holidays, your local public school intends to hold a school play in memory of this great man. The school has already assigned the leading roles and they just need to assign all the other students to filler roles. The filler roles are (in order of importance):

  1. inn visitors
  2. cows
  3. donkeys
  4. sheep
  5. lobsters
  6. propsmen (to design props such as the cradle, etc)

Each year, the school chooses two random students among the students who will be in the play. The first student will be assigned one of the filler roles such that his role is more important than all the other students' roles. The second student will be assigned one of the filler roles less important than the first student's but more important than all the other students' roles. And of course, as what can be inferred, the rest of the students are assigned roles which are less important than the two chosen students.
