Betty has different batter. Each batter is either more bitter or less bitter than any of the other batters. In other words, you will not find any two batters which are as bitter.

Betty arranged bowls in a row, from left to right in such a way that the two leftmost bowls are red and the rest are blue.

Betty then puts a different batter in each of the bowls. Betty thinks it's better if for each of the blue batter-filled bowls, at least one of the two bowls immediately to its left contains a bitterer batter. If this condition is not satisfied, Betty becomes bothered.

How many different ways can Betty put the bitter batters into the bowls such that she does not become bothered?

Explanation

For the first test case, there are two ways to arrange the bowls of bitter batter in a row. Moreover, both bowls are not subject to Betty's condition, so Betty is not bothered. Both arrangements are acceptable.

For the second test case, one of the twenty-six possibilities is if you put the least bitter batter on the first bowl, the bitterest batter on the second bowl, the second bitterest batter on the third, the less bitter among the remaining batter on the fourth and the last one on the fifth.
