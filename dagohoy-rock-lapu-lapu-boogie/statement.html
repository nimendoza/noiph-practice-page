For the first time, the Sugbo-anons and the Bol-anons have agreed to do a dance number together. The dance number will involve dancers lined up in a row, side-by-side facing the audience.

Each dancer will be dancing either the Lapu-Lapu Boogie or the Dagohoy Rock. The Lapu-Lapu Boogie, which is the traditional Sugbo-anon dance, is named after Lapu-Lapu. He was ruler of Mactan who led a battle against Magellan in order to resist Spanish colonization. The Dagohoy Rock, which is the traditional Bol-anon dance, is named after Francisco Dagohoy. He was a Boholano who had led a rebellion against the Spanish which lasted for almost a century.

Of course, since this is a traditional Filipino dance, and not something hip and modern, there will be a lot of critics who will try to find any excuse to call the dance 'lame'.
