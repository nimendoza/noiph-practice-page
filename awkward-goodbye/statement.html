Martin and Martin are two brothers who live in the same town. They go to work in the same zoo and they are both interested in Cryptography. Another thing about them is that they hate having awkward goodbyes. You know those times where you say goodbye to someone and you realize you're walking the same direction? That's very awkward. Martin dislikes it. On the other hand, Martin hates it to death.

In their town, each two places are connected by a unique path. Martin and Martin would usually start going home from the zoo at 5pm. They would initially walk the same path from the zoo until some point where they have to part ways. That's the only time when they say goodbye. Given a map of a village and hypothetical positions where the zoo and the homes of Martin and Martin are, at which cell of the map should they say their goodbyes?
