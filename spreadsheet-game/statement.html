Randy opens up a spreadsheet software. We call a cell occupied if there is an integer on it. Otherwise, the cell is blank. At any point in time, the cursor is in exactly one cell. Using the spreadsheet software, you can do the following things:

  • Use the arrow keys to move the cursor to the adjacent cell in the corresponding direction.
  • Clear a cell: Make a cell blank.
  • Clear a row: Clear all cells in a row.
  • Clear a column: Clear all cells in a column.

Randy fills different cells of the spreadsheet with random integers. Randy fills cells such that the following statements are true:

  • There are no blank cells in between any two occupied cells of the same column.
  • There are no blank cells in between any two occupied cells of the same row.
  • If the cursor is in any of the occupied cells, then one can bring the cursor to any other occupied cell using only the arrow keys without ever bringing the cursor to a blank cell.

(In the screenshot below, you can infer that Randy isn't finished filling it up yet because it doesn't satisfy all of the above statements. )

He then invites his friends, Maxi and Minnie, to play a turn-based game. In a player's turn, she does two actions. Her first action is to pick one column and does exactly one of the following:

  • Clear all the columns to its left.
  • Clear all the columns to its right.

For her second action, she picks a row and does exactly one of the following:

  • Clear all the rows above it.
  • Clear all the rows below it.

After this, she ends her turn. A player's turn is valid if and only if it satisfies both of the following:

  • Cleared at least one occupied cell in the spreadsheet.
  • Ensured that there is at least one occupied cell left.

The game ends as soon as the spreadsheet has exactly one occupied cell left. The value of this last cell will be called the final number. Maxi's goal is to make this last cell as high as possible, and Minnie's goal is to make this last cell as low as possible. In this game, much to the dismay of highly competitive people, everybody wins! There are no winners or losers. No one cares who plays first or last. They just do their best to reach their goal.

If Maxi is the first player to take a turn and both players play optimally to reach their respective objectives, what will the value of the final number be?
