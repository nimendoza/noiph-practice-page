Atoms can either be uncharged, or charged. If they are charged, they are called ions. Each ion carries an integer amount of charge. An anion is an ion that is negatively charged. Similarly, a cation is a positively charged ion. Anions and cations attract each other, and bond to form chemical compounds.

Ann and Cath are doing a chemistry experiment.

Ann has prepared a vat of anions and uncharged atoms. We will more simply represent this vat as a rectangular grid of numbers, where a negative number represents an anion with charge , and a zero represents an uncharged atom.

Cath then injects cations into the vat, one by one, waiting until the current cation successfully bonds with an anion before injecting the next one.

For the specific atoms and molecules that they are studying, Ann and Cath predict that a cation with charge will bond with exactly one free anion with charge . If there are multiple free anions with the right amount of charge, the cation bonds with the anion that is topmost in the rectangular vat. If there are still multiple free anions that are topmost, the cation bonds with the leftmost one. After the cation bonds with the anion, both ions become uncharged and participate in no further chemical interactions.

It is guaranteed that Cath will only inject cations into the vat in such a way that every cation has a matching anion it can bond with.

Ann and Cath want to make a computer simulation of their experiment, in order to find out if their predictions are correct. Given a complete description of Ann's anion vat and the sequence of cations that Cath injects into the vat, they want to find out which anion each cation should bond with, if the experiment behaves according to their prediction. Can you help them?
