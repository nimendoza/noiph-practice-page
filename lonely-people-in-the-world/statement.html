Patrick is a big fan of Air Supply, and his favorite song by them is Two Less Lonely People in the World. Patrick is such a big fan of the song that he wants to make it true. In fact, he wants to do better than the song: he wants to make sure that there will be no more lonely people in the world. First, let us find out what Patrick means by the word lonely.

Almost everyone in the world loves somebody (as eternalized in Dean Martin's song, Everybody Loves Somebody), and most people love more than one person. (Note that love can be one-sided, as Taylor Swift likes to note in You Belong With Me). The amount of love in the world is the sum of the number of people each person in the world loves. If A and B are two different people, we say that person A's love reaches person B if there is a chain of people that starts with person A and ends in person B such that each person loves the next person in the chain. In other words, there exists a chain of people P1, P2, ..., Pn such that P1 = A, Pn = B and Pi loves Pi+1.

Two (different) persons A and B form a loving pair if A's love reaches B and B's love reaches A. A person is lonely if he or she is not part of any loving pair.

Before Patrick can make sure that there will be no more lonely people in the world, Patrick must first find out who the lonely people in the world are. Patrick managed to find out through his good friend Aphrodite who everyone loves. However, Patrick isn't so good at programming, so he needs your help to find out who the lonely people in the world are, given his big list.
