As you should know, the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas released the all-new next generation currency coins last year.

These coins were designed with durability and security in mind, made with durable nickel-plated steel, each coin featuring an electromagnetic signature and micro-printed details using laser-engraving technology. Wow. Such high tech. They feature our beloved national heroes and unique Philippine flora. Wow. Such art. In short, they are matibay, maganda, at mahalaga (durable, beautiful, and valuable).

There's just one tiny problem... The different denominations look too similar and people find them confusing!

Luckily, we have a brilliant solution that enables people to use these coins conveniently, without ruining the beautiful design that teaches people to appreciate their money's worth. It's quite simple really. All we have to do is equip the general public with ultra-high resolution augmented reality eyeglasses that has a special app installed. This app will detect the presence of an NGC coin in the user's visual field, automatically identify the denomination of the coin, and virtually overlay each coin with a more distinctive color, and perhaps even large floating text, that corresponds to its value.

This idea will totally work. There's absolutely no downsides to this. It totally doesn't negate the beautiful NGC designs. And most importantly, it will surely be convenient for the users. After we make the system work, all we'll have to do is to produce and distribute augmented reality glasses to every one in this country. Sounds easy enough. If the public doesn't like it, we can always blame it on their ignorance and release a few infomercials to educate them. Look, they can't even do something as simple as distinguishing coins. It's obviously their problem.

To make the system work, the app will first identify the individual features of the coin, and then classify the coin based on the features detected. For simplicity, we will concern ourselves with only three types of coins---the 1-peso coin, the 5-peso coin, and the 10-peso coin---and three different features---the diameter of the coin, the type of the edge of the coin, and the plant printed on the back side of the coin.

Also for simplicity, we will ignore the fact that the old coins are still in circulation and assume we are always looking at new coins only. Nevermind that reality is much more complicated.

Other members of our engineering team have already figured out how to detect the features, but the feature detection is not perfect. For example, a 5-peso coin will ideally be detected as having a diameter of 25mm, having a plain edge, and having the tayabak plant inscribed on it.

However, the system may wrongly detect the diameter of the coin. It may report that a coin has a diameter of 23mm, has a plain edge, and has the tayabak plant inscribed. In this case, we can still be pretty confident that the coin is indeed a 5-peso coin. Other features may also be wrongly detected. So again, the system may report a coin having a diameter of 25mm, a segmented edge, and having the tayabak plant inscribed. This is still most likely a 5-peso coin.

In general, our rule for determining the denomination of a coin will be as follows: if at least two of the three features of a denomination match the detected features of the coin in question, then we conclude that the coin has that denomination.

Now all that's left to do is to actually determine the most likely denomination of the coin, given the detected features, and this is where you come in.

Input Format

The first line of input will contain one of the following three numbers, representing the detected diameter of the coin in question: 23, 25, or 27.

The second line of input will contain one of the following words, in lowercase English letters, representing the detected edge type of the coin in question: segmented, plain, or reeded.

The third line of input will contain one of the following words, in lowercase English letters, representing the detected plant printed on the back side of the coin in question: waling-waling, tayabak, or kapa-kapa.

Constraints

Subtask 1 (30 points)

The first line of input will contain only either 23 or 25.

The second line of input will contain only either segmented or plain.

The third line of input will contain only either waling-waling or tayabak.

Subtask 2 (70 points)

No additional constraints.

Output Format

Print a single line of output containing an integer representing the value of the coin in question, or APPRECIATE YOUR MONEY if the value of the coin cannot be determined.
