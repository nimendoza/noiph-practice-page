Every day a train with freight cars (labelled to ) would stop to load and unload goods in front of Cheech and Chong's house. Both of them are the children of parents who are fond of names that begin with "CH".

Instead of being annoyed at the freight train every day, the two brothers would instead play a game and it goes as follows: Cheech and Chong would choose a contiguous block of freight cars from to . They choose this such that the number of cars is always divisible by 4. They call this the chosen cars.

Cheech positions himself between the first two chosen cars they chose and yells chooga chooga. He multiplies the weight of both cars and adds it to his score. Chong positions himself between the next two cars and yells choo choo. Just like Cheech, he multiplies the weight of the two cars beside him and adds it to his score. Then Cheech position himself between the next two chosen cars, yells and increases his score as before.

When the freight train arrives everyday, each car contains kilogram worth of goods. Then the workers will load and unload goods on each car, as described in the input. Between these times, Cheech and Chong might start playing this game. The challenge now is to keep track of their scores and identify who wins and by how much.

Input Format

The first line of input contains , the number of test cases. The following lines describe the test cases. Each test case describes one day in Cheech and Chong's world.

The first line of each test case contains two space-separated positive integers and , representing the number of freight cars connected to the train, and the number of activities done during that day respectively.

The next lines each describe the activities of that day in chronological order.

  • If the line is of the form LOAD i w, that means the workers load/unload the goods from the freight car. After doing so, the new weight of the goods in that freight car is kilograms.

  • If the line is of the form GAME i j, that means Cheech and Chong start their game as described in the problem statement, considering the weights of the freight cars at that point in time.

Output Format

Every time Cheech and Chong play a round, (i. e. for each GAME line in the input), you must describe how the game ends.

  • If Cheech wins (or if it's a tie), you need to solve for the non-negative integer where is how many more points Cheech scored than Chong. Output CHEECH c.

  • If Chong wins, you need to solve for the positive integer where is how many more points Chong scored than Cheech. Output CHONG c.

Constraints


It is guaranteed that the number of freight cars for each GAME is a multiple of four.
