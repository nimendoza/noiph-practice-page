Shan and Jerome are playing a brand new exciting game: "Extreme Triangle!" Extreme Triangle is played on a piece of paper with dots placed all over the paper. The players play in turns, one after the other. On the first turn, the first player picks three dots on the paper and records the area of the triangle formed by the three dots. On each player's turn, the player tries to pick three dots such that the area of the triangle formed by the newly chosen dots is greater than the last one. If the player cannot find such a set of three dots, then the previous player is considered to have won and have found the Extreme Triangle.

Shan is very competitive, and wants to win against Jerome. Shan has figured out that if she immediately picks the three dots that form the Extreme Triangle, then Shan immediately wins the game. However, Shan is not good at finding areas of triangles. Please help Shan by writing a program to find the three dots that form the Extreme Triangle.

Input Format

The first line contains a single integer, , which is the number of dots placed on the paper.

The next lines contain the locations of the dots on the paper. Each line contains two integers, and , separated by a space, that represent the Cartesian coordinates of the dot on the paper.

No subset of three dots of the given dots will be collinear, and all given dots will have distinct coordinates.

Output Format

Output a single line containing a single integer: twice the area of the Extreme Triangle. Note that this will always be an integer.
