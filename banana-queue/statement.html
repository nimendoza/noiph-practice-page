Martin and a bunch of his coworkers are to distribute bananas to the monkeys kept in the zoo. However, Martin's coworkers suddenly called in sick one day. Even his brother, Martin, who was named after him seems to be sick that day. And so it was up to Martin to distribute bananas to the monkeys in the zoo.

Unlike people attempting to ride the MRT during the rush hour, the monkeys were very organized and form queues of monkeys each. Normally, each queue would have a worker assigned to it. But today, Martin is doing it all by himself. So, Martin sees in front of him, queues from left to right.

Some monkeys are impatient and some monkeys are not. But they respect queues! No matter how impatient they are, they would never dare take a banana if the monkey in front them hasn't gotten one yet. Each monkey has an impatience score. The higher the score, the more impatient they are. Martin decides to use a greedy algorithm to prevent impatient monkeys from throwing a tantrum. He looks at the front of each queue and gives a banana to the most impatient monkey in front of some queue. The monkey then happily leaves and does a little monkey dance while eating his banana. If two monkeys in front have the same impatience score, Martin chooses the first one from his left.

Little did Martin know that every time a monkey leaves, the monkeys left in the queues grow bitter. The bitterness they harness depends on their position in their respective queue.
