Shannon Joseph and Mariah Timantha are very good friends who like literature. They read haikus, sonnets, limericks, epics, awits and other literary forms.

An awit, for example has four lines per stanza. Florante at Laura for example, is an awit with 399 stanzas. That's a total of 1596 lines. One day, they decided that they too, should make their own poetry form!

As they are very creative, they decide to call their new poetry form Awit2DARemixYo!. However, they haven't decided how many lines per stanza and how many stanzas in total the first Awit2DARemixYo! will contain.

Since Mariah Timantha likes to complicate things, she proposes that each write random numbers in pieces of paper (not necessarily distinct) and put them in two different urns, a gold urn for Shannon and a white urn for Timantha. Some argued that the urns were black and blue but we're not going to talk about this since it will be a waste of time. Anyway, they will then each randomly take an integer from both urns. The number they get from the gold urn will be the number of lines per stanza their first Awit2DARemixYo! will contain, and the number from the white urn will be the number of stanzas.

Shannon, being the more informed of the two when it comes to literature, tells Timantha that they must limit the number of lines so as not to overwhelm (or underwhelm) the reader. Timantha, being mathematically inclined, proposes that and be the lower bound and upper bound of the total number of lines, respectively.

They now realize that they can't do their initial plan anymore since what they draw might mean that the resulting Awit2DARemixYo! would not fall within and total lines. Out of the possible ways they can take a ball from the two urns, how many of those combinations will consequently give them an Awit2DARemixYo! whose number of lines are within the bounds they declared?
