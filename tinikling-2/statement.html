After recovering having an existential crisis since she kept on pondering to herself if she is human or she is dancer, Cleverlyn decides to study different Philippine dances. I guess she realized she was the latter.

Cleverlyn's thesis was about Tinikling. Tinikling is a traditional dance in the Philippines. It usually involves two people holding bamboo poles. They will tap them on the ground and sometimes slam them together. Dancers must step in between the poles and not get caught when the bamboo poles bang each other. Needless to say, this requires perfect footing.

Now, since Cleverlyn was very clever, her thesis involved a modified version of Tinikling where it involved more people and some sort of challenge. She calls it Tinikling 2 because she was the most clever at giving names to things she had invented.

In this version of Tinikling, the dancers must be extra careful with their footing. When the music starts, dancers alternately step left and right. This is mandatory. Otherwise, they will get boo-ed off the dance floor.

There are designated areas in the dance floor. The people holding a bamboo are in a very complicated layout and it takes an expert to be able to go around the dance floor without one's ankles getting squeezed in between the bamboo. Not only must their feet not get caught by the moving bamboo, they must have what Cleverlyn calls ending gracefully. By ending gracefully, we mean that the dancer's last step must be using the foot he/she started with.
