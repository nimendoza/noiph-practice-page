Despite the extreme negative backlash Battleback II: Electric Boogaloo received, the game topped all sales charts and became an instant bestseller. Ethically Ambiguous, the game's publisher, exceeded sales expectations for the quarter, and so they have decided to continue using the successful microtransaction model in all their future games.

The next game they have planned is Extreme Ambulation. In this game, you control a character who must complete quests located across the world.

The world can be represented as an infinite 2D grid. The first quest always happens at the player's hometown, at the centre of the grid. Pressured to release as soon as possible, Ethically Ambiguous did not want to spend too much time and money designing many different locations, and so each quest happens at a location adjacent to the previous one; the next quest is either one unit north (), south (), west () or east () from the location of the current one.

They still had to design too many locations, however. They eventually realized that, by having some quests be at the same location, they can reuse locations and hence spend less time making locations for the vanilla game, and spend more time making locations that can be unlocked only by playing the game for 40 hours (or by paying extra). Indeed, the intent is to provide players with a sense of pride and accomplishment for unlocking different locations.
