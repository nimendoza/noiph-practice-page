You've just arrived at your country's airport from your travels abroad. And you currently have pieces of balikbayan boxes, of which box # has dollars worth of items inside. (The boxes are indexed .) You double-checked the rules and regulations and you're sure that none of these should be declared at the customs area of the airport. Since the bureau of customs is on the lookout for smuggled firearms and drugs in balikbayan boxes (which totally happens according to their records), the boxes you brought may be subject to inspection as you navigate your way around the airport.

You start at one of the terminals of the airport and at every hall to a different room, there is a customs representative, each having a different set of criteria for what can and cannot be brought out into the country. For example, a representative may confiscate a box containing pencils you brought home for your siblings because they are imported and needed to be monitored for tax and security purposes. And so each balikbayan box containing pencils will need to be left to them for security reasons.

The challenge is this: you must make your way from your starting point to the exit where your family awaits you. You have anticipated that you will lose boxes along the way, and so you optimize your path such that the set of boxes you have upon reaching the exit has the highest value possible. Moreover, you have to be with all your (unconfiscated) boxes at all times. You have no intention to leave them lying around the airport unattended! You fear that the wonderful elves at the airport might give you surprise gifts. And you don't like surprises. And gifts. And elves.

But there's another problem. You don't exactly know which rooms the starting room and the exit room are! Instead, you have a few candidate pairs of starting and exit rooms, and you have to solve the problem for each of these candidate pairs.
