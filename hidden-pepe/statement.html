Jose's name is a shortened version of Joseph. Joseph is derived from Giuseppe, whose last two letters are pe. His parents are Filipino and Filipinos find repeated names cute. This is why his nickname is Pepe.

Pepe usually meets up with his friends Lotlot, Tohntohn, and Plutarcoplutarco. Recently, he realized that all of them have a "base" which is repeated exactly once. The "base" of his friends' names are "Lot", "Tohn" and "Plutarco". Fascinated by this phenomenon, Pepe called these types of names repeaters.

To entertain himself, he would hide the name of his friends by adding letters around their names. For example, if he wanted to hide his name, he could end up with the string SPRUKEPECS. Here, he added the letters S, R, U, K, and C around his name. Moreover, this string has a special property that the name he hid (which is PEPE) is the longest repeater hidden in the string. Take note that one can erase S-RUKE-ECS and find the hidden repeater PP. However, we all know that PEPE is longer than PP. And so, PP is not the hidden name.

After doing it for his name, he also did it for his friends' names. Same rules applied. The hidden name is one of the longest repeaters hidden in the string. If the longest repeater is not unique, this means that Pepe's friend has multiple variations of their name. In this case, output any one of them. Given a list of strings that Pepe made, can you figure out the names of his friends?

Input Format

The first line of input contains a single integer , the number of friends Pepe has.

The next lines contain a string consisting of uppercase letters. This is the string Pepe acquired by adding letters around his one of his friend's name, as discussed in the problem statement.

Explanation

Pepe is friends with himself and so in the first case, he hid his name like that, as discussed in the problem statement. Two variations of Pepe's fifth friend's name (the one on the sixth test case) are Bebe and Bibi. Hence, printing either is acceptable. Note that Pepe's fifth friend's name may have other variations not mentioned in this explanation.

Finally, Pepe seems to have "cheated" by not having a hidden name on the fifth and seventh line. We say 2hidden4me as an edgy way to tell Pepe that we know about his tricks. During the second time this happens, we say 3hidden5me, incrementing the integers every time this happens.
