The king and queen of Rock and Roll (respectively) recently acquired paintings and plan to put all of them in a single art room. They hired a gifted interior designer, Jojette, to make a floor plan for their art room. They're not entirely sure if they should've hired an interior designer. Perhaps they should've hired an architect instead. But she loves Rock and Roll and that's a good enough for the royal couple to hire her. They also hired you to be the royal programmer to solve their problem explained below.

Even though they were able to agree on which interior designer to choose, the couple was known to have a lot of differences. This is why subjects are starting to wonder if they'll last as a couple. Even a few of their differences manifested during the planning phase of the art room!

The king and queen of Rock and Roll have varying attention for details.

The king of Rock, not very meticuIous with detai|s, rated his most favorite artwork as rank , his second most favorite as rank all the way down to rank . And so, we index the artworks by these numbers.

The queen of Roll was a little bit more detail-oriented than the king of Rock. She would rate the artworks on a scale of to . The higher the score, the more beautiful she finds them.

The king and queen of Rock and Roll visit the art room in different ways.

The king of Rock once read in a programming contest that to traverse a room and see everything, one must simply follow the left side of the wall. Note that he just uses the wall as a guide, but he still faces forward as he walks. After entering the art room, he stops and looks at the first painting, then follows the left wall again. He stops at the next painting that he has never seen before and follows the left wall to see the next painting.

The queen of Roll simply goes inside the art room, looks at the first artwork she sees then chooses one of two directions (left or right). Upon making a decision, she must see an artwork at the end of each hall she chooses. She repeats this process until she arrives at a dead end. Note that this dead end should still have an artwork but no where else to turn. Upon reaching a dead end, she commands her servants to blindfold her (so that she can internalize the beauty of the last painting she saw) and to escort her out of the room. Apart from her sticker sheet collection, this was the only other thing subjects find strange about her.

The king and queen of Rock and Roll intend to experience art differently.

The king of Rock insists that he sees all artworks in order from best to worst (according to his ratings).

The queen of Roll insists that after the first artwork, every subsequent artwork must be better (according to her ratings) than all the artworks she saw during that visit.

Your task as the royal programmer of Rock and Roll is to figure out which painting should be visible when turning left or right from each painting, subject to the constraints of the royal couple. Then, you will send this plan to Jojette, the interior designer.

Jojette, having a bachelor and masters degree at being an interior designer from the School and University of Rock and Roll (respectively), also has some constraints of her own. This is mostly her problem but it didn't stop her from discussing it with you anyway. According to her, artwork must only be hanged at the end of a hall. Moreover, the path can only turn left or turn right (or both) at the end of a hall with an artwork on it. Finally, there must be one and only one entrance and this should lead to the king of Rock's favorite painting.

Given all these constraints, figure out what to send to Jojette so that she will be able to design a floor plan which pleases both the King and Queen of Rock and Roll.
