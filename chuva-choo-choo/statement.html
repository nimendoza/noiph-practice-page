During the ancient times, to show that you have a crush on someone, you give them a cassette tape with songs. The tradition was to assemble a playlist of songs your crush likes and put it on the cassette tape. The last song on the playlist should be a special song which summarizes what you feel for your crush.

You haven't decided which songs you'll place apart from the special last one. And for this, you've decided that the song should be able to convey to your crush that you love to hold hands in Katips with him/her, that you are deeply inlabs forever with him/her, and that you wish to be his/her special someone. Of course, this song is the all-time classic - Chuva Choo Choo by Jolina.

You have assembled a list of other songs that you wish to put before this special song. It doesn't matter if they're cheesy or jologs, as long as it's from your heart. You intend to have at least seconds worth of music before Chuva Choo Choo plays to create suspense when your crush listens to the tape. However, you don't want the playlist preceding the special song to be too long. Otherwise, your crush might think you're a boring person. So, you decide that the length of the songs preceding Chuva Choo Choo should be at most seconds in total.

How many ways can you choose the other songs such that it fits within your constraints?

Note that the order of the songs before Chuva Choo Choo does not matter, and that you can only place a song in the list at most once.
