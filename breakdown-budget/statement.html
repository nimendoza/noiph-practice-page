Each carriage has a safety index, represented by an integer . The safer the carriage, the higher the safety index.

Today, Harry took the first train alone to show that he understands the everyday plight of commuters. Harry's train did not experience any problems. The rest of the commuters took the second train. Surprisingly, this train experiences a breakdown on the way to every station. This means that there are breakdowns.

The th breakdown happens on the way to station for . A breakdown means that a consecutive set of carriages detached from the main train. More precisely, the detached carriages are the ones between carriage and carriage (inclusive). Repairmen arrive to fix the issue and an inspector arrives to look at the detached carriages and make a report.

If the reported sum of the safety index of the detached carriages on a particular breakdown is negative, then the Department of Transportation can be sued. Otherwise, the train line is declared fit to run and everything goes on as planned after the repairmen do their thing.
