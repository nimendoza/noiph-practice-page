The Kingdom of Jabahw existed a long time ago. It was ruled by a king, King Rodrix XXX, son of King Rodrix XXIX. At first he did not want to be king, but then he decided he wanted to become one. His mind was very fickle.

Jabahw was a very peaceful land because of a group of vigilantes, called the Jabahwokees, was secretly rewarded by the king everytime a criminal was gotten rid of. Moreover, any sort of justice system wasn't invented yet. So, when someone you love looks like one of the wanted criminals, they might get killed. It was truly a time in the distant past, a time when the only solution for peace and order was to kill all criminals. People back then think of criminals as monsters, instead of humans who had mental or emotional problems. This is why they thought it was justified.

Even though they lack due process on determining whether someone was innocent or not, they did have a process on how to report criminals. It took them around three to six months to figure this out, but they were able to make a form which asks where the crime was committed, and how many hours ago it was committed, and a sketch of the criminal.

How do we represent the location in this problem? Imagine the kingdom to be a grid which was square kilometers big. Each cell is a square kilometer and is identified by which row and column it appears on the map. For example, the north-westernmost cell is in the first row and first column of a properly oriented map of the kingdom.

Two cells are said to be adjacent if they share an edge. It is assumed that the fastest time you can travel between two adjacent cells is one hour. The location on the reports only tell which cell the crime was committed in. When a crime is reported, the Jabahwokees search for the criminal on all the cells humanely possible for the criminal to reach. Note that the cells where the criminal may be hiding in is limited by the amount of time elapsed between the crime and the time it was reported. Moreover, just to be sure, the vigilantes kill EXACTLY one person in each of these cells. Usually a person which slightly matches the description of the criminal is killed. Normally, with this process, they are able to kill the criminal and restore peace in Jabahw.

There was also a group of rebels who keep track of how much people were killed. They do not like that innocent people have to be killed to bring peace to their kingdom. They have a secret network and they report to each other how many people were killed in certain areas. They also keep a record of all the crime reports to compute these numbers. Their goal is that eventually people will come to their senses and see that a lot of blood is being shed as the price of peace and order.

You love your kingdom and its people very much and so you decide to join the rebels in accumulating the data. For now, though, the data must remain hidden amongst yourselves, as the citizens will just say that these documents are negative campaigning and black propaganda, only made to tarnish the reputation of the king. Hopefully, in the future, when people are much more rational and civilized, they would look at these data and vow to never let such a king rule in this manner ever again.

Input Format

The first line of input contains a single integer , the number of lines following it.

The next lines of input can be any of the following two formats:

  • CRIME AT i j h HRS AGO, meaning that hours ago, a crime was committed at row and column .

  • KILL COUNT AT i j, meaning somebody wants to know how many people have been killed at row and column so far.
