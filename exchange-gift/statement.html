Barbie and Jak are having an exchange gift this Christmas. This is a very important event and so many news outlets reported their story. So many of their followers were dismayed to find out that their gift-giving might not proceed. This is because Jak's gift for Barbie may not be functioning as expected.

Jak plans to give Barbie a box of dimensions , with holes along the top and bottom, one hole on the far left side, and one hole on the far right side. Each cell in the box is either blank or has a reflector.

A ball can be thrown into the box from any of the holes. It moves straight into the direction it is thrown into until it hits a reflector. When it hits a reflector, the ball reflects from its surface, and moves into the new direction. Then the reflector switches orientation, from / to \.

An example of such a box, when , is shown in the first figure of the following diagram. The second figure shows Jak throwing a ball into hole T4. It goes down one cell and hits the reflector, and then moves to the right. It keeps moving right until it hits another reflector, and then it moves up again, leaving the box through the hole T6. After it leaves the box, the reflectors are positioned as in the third figure.

Now, Jak throws a ball into a hole and records which hole it comes out of. He does this times. Given this information, Jak wants to know a possible initial layout of the box. Otherwise, the box is broken, and Barbie and Jak will have to exchange their love instead.

Input Format

The first line of input contains and , the dimension of the box and the number of balls thrown, separated by a space.

The th of the next lines consist of four space-separated tokens, representing the th ball thrown. The first two tokens represent the hole which the ball is thrown into, and the last two tokens represent the hole from which it comes out of. A hole is thus identified by a pair of tokens. The first of these two tokens is either T, R, B or L, representing the top, right, bottom, and left sides of the box. The second is an integer from to (inclusive), representing the number of the hole along that side. There is only hole on either the left side or the right side of the box, and the holes on the top and bottom are numbered from left to right. It is guaranteed that each pair of tokens represents a valid hole.

Balls never come out of the left side or the right side of the box.

Subtask 4 (12 points)

Jak never throws balls into the left side or the right side of the box.

Balls never come out of the left side or the right side of the box.

Subtask 5 (36 points)

Subtask 6 (12 points)

No additional constraints.

Output Format

Output a single line containing a string of length , representing a possible initial layout of the box. The th character of this string is . if there is no reflector in the th leftmost cell of the box. Otherwise, it is / or \, depending on the orientation of the reflector.

If there are multiple possible valid layouts, any one will be accepted.

If such a layout cannot exist, output LOVE IS ALL WE NEED FOR CHRISTMAS.

T 4 T 6

Sample Output 0

Explanation 0

Refer to the illustration above.

It is easy to see that the box is consistent with the observed behavior of the balls.

  • The first ball is put into the 4th hole of the top side of the box. It comes out of the 6th hole of the top side.
  • The second ball is also put into the 4th hole of the top side of the box. Since the reflector on the 4th cell of the box has changed to /, it moves leftwards instead and exits through the 2nd hole of the top side.
  • The third ball is put into the far right side of the box. Since the reflector on the 6th cell of the box has changed to \, it comes out of the 6th hole of the top side.
