Every year on the 15th of May, the citizens of Lucban, Quezon celebrate their very unique and very colorful festival, the Pahiyas Festival. Each household decorates the exterior of their home using paper mâchés and other decorations of vibrant colors.

A local rice wafer called "kiping" is also served. Kiping the tradition of recent years, the city hall hands out kiping of different colors.

The order in which the different colored kipings are served are represented by a string consisting of uppercase and lowercase letters. The lowercase letters represent one color while the upper case letters represent a sequence of colors.

In particular, the personnel from the city hall read the string character-by-character from left to right. If the character they are reading is a lowercase letter, they hand out a kiping whose color is represented by that letter. If the character is an uppercase letter, they start reading the string represented by that letter. If they reach the end of any string, they return to reading the string which they were originally reading from. The festival ends if they finish reading the string .
