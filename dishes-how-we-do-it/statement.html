After his father burned all the bridges in his archipelago consisting of islands, Prince Manuel VI attempted to reconnect his archipelago kingdom by building bridges. Unfortunately, the kingdom does not have a lot of budget for bridges because most of the kingdom's budget is allocated to state universities and research facilities. And so, he made it mandatory that each of the islands (except his island of residency, since he's the prince) to chip in and sponsor the construction of one bridge. There is also a possibility that citizens would offer to sponsor the construction of a bridge.

Once the budget has been settled, Prince Manuel VI insisted that each island must be reachable from any other island just by crossing any series of bridges. It shouldn't be necessary to ride ferries to go to any island within the archipelago. And once the plan was finalized, the bridges were then constructed.

It took years and years but then the bridges were finally constructed. King Manuel VI (he became a king after the long construction) decreed that there should be a festival in celebration of the newly-constructed bridges.
