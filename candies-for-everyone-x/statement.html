There are students sitting on a long bench. Initially, each one holds some number of candies. The students are numbered 1 to from left to right, and the student (), initially has candies.

It's halloween! Every now and then, the teacher brings some amount of candies, gives them all to some student, and asks him/her to take some and pass the rest to the right. However, students like candies, so instead of getting one and passing the rest to the right, he/she instead takes exactly half of the candies and passes the rest to the right! If there are an odd number of candies, he/she takes exactly one more than what he/she gives to the right. This process repeats for the next person, and so on, proceeds until there are no more candies to pass, or there are no more children to the right, in which case the last person holding the candies will take them for himself/herself.

Also, every now and then, a student looks to the right and counts how many candies in total the students to the right have.

Your job is to print how many candies a student sees whenever he/she looks to the right.

Input Format

The first line contains two integers and , separated by a space, which is the number of students and the number of events to process, respectively.

The next line contains integers , separated by single spaces, where is the number of candies student initially has.

The next lines contain the events. Each line contains exactly one event. There are two types of events:

  • GIVE i V, which represents the event that a teacher brings candies, gives them all to student , and tells student to take some and pass the rest to the right.
  • LOOK i, which represents the event that student looks to the right and counts the number of candies he/she sees.

No lines follow the line containing the last event.

Output Format

For each event of the second type (LOOK i), you have to output a line containing the number of candies the student sees.

Sample Input

LOOK 4
LOOK 7
LOOK 2

LOOK 2
GIVE 2 10
LOOK 2
GIVE 6 10
LOOK 6

Sample Output

52
0
162
163
168
7

Explanation

LOOK 4 means student 4 looks to the right. He/she sees 50 + 0 + 2 = 52 candies. Therefore, we output 52. Note that the 100 candies that student 4 holds are not counted.

LOOK 7 means student 7 looks to the right. He/she sees no candies (because he/she is the last student). Therefore, we output 0.

LOOK 2 is similar, and student 2 sees 10 + 100 + 50 + 0 + 2 = 162 candies.

GIVE 5 1 means that the teacher gives one candy to student 5. Since there is only one candy, student 5 takes one and has no more candies to pass. After this operation, student 5 now has 51 candies. Note that no line will be output by this event.

For LOOK 2, student 2 looks again to the right. This time, he/she sees 10 + 100 + 51 + 0 + 2 = 163 candies.

GIVE 2 10 means that the teacher gives ten candies to student 2. So student 2 takes five and passes the other five to student 3. Then student 3 takes three candies and passes the remaining two to student 4. Now student 4 takes one and passes the remaining one to student 5. Finally, student 5 keeps the last candy. After this operation, the students now have, respectively, 0, 10, 13, 101, 52, 0, and 2 candies.

For LOOK 2, student 2 looks to the right and sees 13 + 101 + 52 + 0 + 2 = 168 candies.

GIVE 6 10 means that the teacher gives ten candies to student 6. So student 6 takes five and passes the other five to student 7. At this point, student 7 has no more to pass candies to, so he keeps those five. After this operation, the students now have, respectively, 0, 10, 13, 101, 52, 5, and 7 candies.

Finally, for LOOK 6, student 6 looks to the right and sees 7 candies.
