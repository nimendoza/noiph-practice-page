Johnny is having a strange dream. He is in one of the cells of an grid-shaped room with no walls. He has no mobility. That is, he cannot move.

He has updogs, downdogs, leftdogs and rightdogs. Johnny understands that using one rightdog transports him to the cell on his immediate right. Using one leftdog transports him to the cell on his immediate left. A similar phenomenon goes for the updogs and downdogs.

Johnny does not want this dream to end in a nightmare. This dream will end up being a nightmare if one of the following happens:

  • He goes off the room.
  • He hits one of the Lisa clones.

You see, the room he's moving in contains Lisa clones. If he ends up in the same cell as one of the Lisa clones, then he automatically hits that Lisa clone. That Lisa clone will subsequently tear him apart.

Updogs, downdogs, leftdogs and rightdogs are types of dogs. Johnny is told that he cannot wake up unless he uses exactly of the dogs.

If he manages to use exactly of the dogs without turning the dream into a nightmare, Johnny will be able to wake up in order to say "hi" to his friend Mark and truthfully say that "I did not hit her!", where Lisa is the antecedent of the pronoun her.

How many ways can Johnny use the dogs he has such that he wakes up from a dream and not a nightmare? Note that he must decide to use which dogs to use and in which order to use them.

Since the answer may be very large, only output it modulo .

Input Format

The first line of input contains two space-separated integers and .

The next lines each contain a string of characters. Each of these strings contain only the characters ., L or J. These lines make up the grid.

  • . represents a cell that doesn't contain a Lisa clone.
  • L represents a cell that contains a Lisa clone.
  • J represents Johnny's starting position. There is only a single J in these lines. Johnny's starting position does not contain a Lisa clone.

The last line of input contains five space-separated integers , , , , .

Explanation 0

In the dream in the sample test data, Johnny has two leftdogs and two downdogs, and he needs to use exactly of them to wake up. Shown above are the two sequences that he could take in order to escape the dream successfully. Note that both solutions use two downdogs and one leftdog, but are counted as separate ways due to order. We thus output , which is just 2.

Shown above are two ways to use his dogs that would unfortunately result in a nightmare. In the left case, Johnny ends up passing through a cell containing a Lisa clone at some point, so it fails. In the right case, Johnny exits the boundaries of the room, and it is forbidden to do so at any point in time, so it fails as well.
