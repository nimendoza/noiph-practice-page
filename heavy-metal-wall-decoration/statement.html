King met his boyfriend Harry when he was attending a heavy metal concert. They discovered soon enough that not only were they fans of heavy metal music, they were both also collectors of heavy metal pipes. Together, they collect four different shapes of heavy metal pipes:

1   ..X.       2 .XX.       3 .XXX.       4 ..XX.
    .XX.         .X..         .X...         .XX..
    ....         .XX.         .X...         .X...

The cells marked X in the visualizations above determine which cells of a grid are covered by these heavy metal pipes.

The couple initially wanted to cover an square area of their living room wall using only pieces from their collection. However, King decided that they should hang a photo of themselves somewhere in this grid just to have a personal flair. In particular, if we consider the area as a grid of unit squares, exactly one of the squares must contain the photo and the rest should be covered by one of the four types of pipes they collect.

Given the dimensions of the square area they wish to cover and which cell the photo is placed, determine one way they can cover the rest of the wall with the pipes in their collection. Note that the couple can choose to rotate or flip the heavy metal pipes to fit better.

Input Format

The first line of input contains , the number of test cases. The following lines describe the test cases.

Each test case consists of a single line containing three space-separated integers , and in that order describing the situation. In this case, the couple wants to cover an grid where their photo together is placed on the cell which is on the th row and th column.

Output Format

For each test case, if the wall is impossible to cover, output two lines, the first line contains the string IMPOSSIBLE and the second line is an empty line.

Otherwise, output lines with characters each and then a single empty line. The first lines represent the grid using the following format:

  • The th character of the th line shall be #. This represents the cell with the photo.
  • Represent the area covered by a particular pipe using a single uppercase letter. To be able to differentiate against different pipes, adjacent cells that are not covered by the same pipe must have different letters.

Sample Input

3
2 1 2
3 1 3
7 2 6

Sample Output

O#
OO

AA#
BAA
BBA

KKOOKKK
KEORK#K
VEERRII
VVGGSSI
KKKGOSJ
KRIIOOJ
KRRIJJJ

Explanation

In the first test case, the photo is placed on the first row and second column of the grid. Rotating the first type of pipe (as it appears on the problem statement) 90 degrees clockwise, we are able to fit it in the grid.

Observe in the second and third test case that each set of contiguous letters represent one of the four shapes rotated or flipped around. You may freely use whatever letters you wish as long as the different pieces are distinguished.

For example, changing the E to K will not work in the third output because the upper right corner will seem to have a piece shaped like:

KK.
KK.
.KK

But this is an invalid piece. In fact, these are two different type 1 pipes, adjacent to each other. Representing them by the same uppercase letter renders them indistinguishable.
