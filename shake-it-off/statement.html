After breaking up with her first boyfriend, Taylor frequently stayed out too late to distract herself. Because of the sadness, she usually got nothing in her brain. At least that's what people say. But she keeps cruising. After all, in life, you just can't stop moving.

As part of her process of moving on, she bought a sticker sheet to commemorate her first relationship. It has rows and columns and each cell contains a sticker. To figuratively show she was incomplete, she removed a random sticker from the stickers on her sheet. It wouldn't immediately detach, so she had to... shake it off. She thought of this as a metaphor that she must make a conscious effort to shake off the negative feelings and tell herself that everything's gonna be alright.

From that moment on, Taylor believed that heartbreakers are always going to break her heart, just like her haters who hate and the fakers who fake.

To remind herself that she must shake off melancholy feelings, she promised herself that whenever she gains a new ex boyfriend, she will remove a sticker from the sheet. However, this couldn't be just a completely random sticker. She had some criteria for it.

  • Every relationship she will have is special. Hence, she tells herself she mustn't remove a sticker which is adjacent to a sticker she has already removed. By adjacent we mean that it should not share a common edge or corner with one of the blank spaces she previously made.

  • Every relationship she will have was focused. She never puts her baby in the corner. And thus, she decided to never remove a sticker on any of the edges or corners of the sheet. That is, she will never remove a sticker from the leftmost and rightmost column and also never remove a sticker from the top and bottom row. This includes the very first sticker she removed.

How many more exes can she afford until she runs out of stickers to shake off the sheet (in accordance to her two rules discussed above)?
