Seok-woo got stuck in the Carriage of a cargo train filled with zombies. His daughter, Soo-an, got stuck in Carriage , the last carriage. She is very scared and wants to see her father. To ease her stress, she continuously sings Aloha O'e until they meet again.

Seok-woo found blueprints of the train and where each obstacle is. It turns out that because how the cargo is arranged, zombies inside the carriages cannot access some areas. These are called safe spaces. In particular, carriage is a completely safe area. However, Seok-woo is still worried about his daughter.

Hence, Seok-woo decided that he has to risk his life go through all carriages. He looks at the blueprints carefully, looking at how much space of each carriage is safe. If an area has a lot of safe space, he can relax and rest a bit. On the other hand, if an area has not so much safe space, he has to be alert at all times. A zombie might try to bite his hand and cause him to never see his daughter again!

Sometimes, some cargo in a carriage get randomly cleared. This lessens the safe space for the carriage. After every time an obstacle is removed, can you figure out how much safe space is left in each carriage?

Input Format

The first line of input contains , the number of carriages Seok-woo has to go through. The following lines then describe the carriages one by one.

The first line describing a carriage contains three space-separated positive integers , and where is the length and is the width of the accessible area of the carriage in meters, as shown in Seok-woo's blueprints.

The next lines draw the map. Each of the lines contains a string of length consisting of the following characters:

  • # means that there is an obstacle in that space.
  • Z means that there is a zombie in that space.
  • . means that it is an accessible space without a zombie.

Each character represents a square meter area. Note that a zombie cannot move diagonally nor go outside the carriage.

The next lines describe in what order the obstacles are removed. The th line contains two space-separated integers and . This means that the th obstacle removed was the one which was represented by the th character on the th line. It is guaranteed that these coordinates point to an obstacle.

Explanation

For the first carriage:

  • Removing the obstacle in does not give the zombie more space because it cannot move diagonally. And so, counting the newly opened space, there is square meters of safe space.
  • Removing the obstacle in lets the zombie access , and . Hence, there will be square meters of safe space left.
  • Removing the obstacle in lets the zombie access the rest of the carriage. Hence there is no safe space left.

For the second carriage:

  • There is initially no safe space. Removing the obstacle in does not add a safe space either since the zombie can easily access it.
  • Removing the obstacle in generates a new safe space. Hence, there is exactly one square meter of safe space after removing that obstacle.

The case of the third carriage is easily understood.
