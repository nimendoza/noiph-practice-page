In Tower Bloxx, you start with a vacant lot, and stack up floors to form a building. A lot can have at most one stack of floors (i.e. a building).

At the start of the game, you place a floor and then stack the next floor on top of the last one you placed. Do this enough times and you end up with a building with floors and the game congratulates you for this.

However, the game is not finished yet! There's a twist! Apparently, the floors were numbered (for ground floor), , , , and so on. There shall be no floor labelled number due to silly superstitions designed to encourage time-pressured contestants to carefully avoid off-by-one errors.

The game showed you the labels just now and they are stacked in a random order!

The correct way to stack the floors is that the ground floor (G) is the bottom-most floor of the building. On top of that is the second floor (2), etc. If , you might have inferred that directly on top of the twelfth floor (12) will be the fourteenth floor (14).

Now, how exactly does the game propose you fix this? It opens up two vacant lots for you to temporarily work with. You can modify the buildings using the following type of operation:

  • Remove the topmost floor of one (non-empty) lot and place it as the topmost floor of a different (possibly empty) lot.

We say that a lot is non-empty if it has at least one floor on it. Otherwise, we say it is empty (or vacant). The two new lots should be empty after all your operations.

In this task, the input will be the initial order of the floors. Your task is to output the sequence of operations to be done so that the order of the floors on the original lot is correct and the two added lots are empty. Your score is determined by the number of operations to be done. The fewer, the better.

If the original stack was 2 3 4 G from top to bottom, then one way we can fix it as follows:

  • Remove the topmost floor (2) from lot O and place it as the topmost floor of lot X.
  • Remove the topmost floor (3) from lot O and place it as the topmost floor of lot Y.
  • Remove the topmost floor (4) from lot O and place it as the topmost floor of lot Y.
  • Remove the topmost floor (2) from lot X and place it as the topmost floor of lot O.
  • Remove the topmost floor (4) from lot Y and place it as the topmost floor of lot X.
  • Remove the topmost floor (3) from lot Y and place it as the topmost floor of lot X.
  • Remove the topmost floor (3) from lot X and place it as the topmost floor of lot O.
  • Remove the topmost floor (4) from lot X and place it as the topmost floor of lot O.

At the end of this, lot O will have floors 4 3 2 G from top to bottom, as desired and the two other lots are empty.
