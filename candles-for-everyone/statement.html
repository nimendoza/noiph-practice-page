There are students sitting on a long bench, waiting for their teacher to arrive. The students are numbered to from left to right, arranged according to their class number as how their teacher would have wanted. Even though their teacher is celebrating her birthday today, none of them thought of bringing candles.

To remedy the situation of not having candles, the principal brings some amount of candles. For this problem, the amount of candles she gives is always a power of . She gives them all to a student of her choosing, and asks this student to take some and pass the rest either to the left or to the right. However, students like candles, so instead of getting one and passing the rest to the direction instructed by the principal, students instead takes exactly half of the candles and passes the rest to the appropriate direction! If a student receives an odd number of candles, this student takes exactly one more than what he/she gives to the next student. This process repeats for the next person, and so on, proceeds until there are no more candles to pass, or there are no more children to pass the candles to, in which case the last person holding the candles will take them for themselves.

Also, every now and then, a student looks to the left or right and counts how many candles in total the students in that direction have. Your job is to print how many candles a student sees whenever he/she looks to the left or right.

Input Format

The first line contains two integers and , separated by a space, which is the number of students and the number of events to process, respectively. Initially, each student has candles.

The next lines contain the events. Each line describes exactly one event. There are two types of events:

  • GIVE LEFT i k, which represents the event that a teacher brings candles, gives them all to student , and tells student to take some and pass the rest to the left, as explained in the problem statement.

  • GIVE RIGHT i k, which represents the event that a teacher brings candles, gives them all to student , and tells student to take some and pass the rest to the right, as explained in the problem statement.

  • LOOK LEFT i, which represents the event that student looks to the left and counts the number of candles he/she sees.

  • LOOK RIGHT i, which represents the event that student looks to the right and counts the number of candles he/she sees.

No lines follow the line containing the last event.

Output Format

For each LOOK event, you have to output a line containing the number of candles the student sees. Since the number can be very large, output it modulo .
