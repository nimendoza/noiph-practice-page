We model the topography of a particular area in Athens as an grid. Each cell in this grid is represented by either , or , depending on its height in meters.

If two cells on the grid share an edge and are of the same height, then we say that they are adjacent. A maximal contiguous set of adjacent cells of height is called a plateau.

One can alter the height of any cell by either increasing the height of a cell by meter or decrease it by meter. The height of a cell can be altered at most once.

Socrates' student likes to work in plateaus. Socrates wonders if he could alter the landscape in order to change the number of plateaus in the area. This was what he is occupied with today.

But wait, THERE'S MOORE, Thomas Moore! Thomas adds a constraint to Socrates' question. He insists that the height of all the cells, in meters, should be even after altering it.

Following Moore's extra constraint, Socrates wants the answers to the following questions:

  • What is the minimum number of plateaus that can be formed by altering the heights of the cells?
  • What is the maximum number of plateaus that can be formed by altering the heights of the cells?

In addition, their friend Hitchens also wants proof that such a number of plateaus can be achieved. And the burden of proof is on you! In addition to answering the two questions, you must show how the grid must be altered to achieve the minimum and maximum number.

Scoring

This problem has an unusual scoring system.

For each subtask:

  • If you correctly compute all minimum and maximum numbers, then you get all points for that subtask.
  • Otherwise, if you correctly compute all minimum numbers or all maximum numbers, then you get half of the points for that subtask.
  • Otherwise, you get no points from that subtask.

Note that you also get points for the subtask if your output doesn't follow the output format. Thus, you should always output two valid grids with their correct plateau counts, even if one of them is not the correct minimum or maximum; otherwise, you get points.

Output Format

For each test case, output the minimum and maximum number and the respective grids that achieve the minimum and maximum number. Specifically, the first line must contain the minimum number, and the next lines must describe the grid that achieves the minimum number. Then, the next line must contain the maximum number, and the next lines must describe the grid that achieves the maximum number.

The grids must be outputted in the same format as the input.
