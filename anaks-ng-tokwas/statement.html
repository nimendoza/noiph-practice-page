Tofus have a strange way of reproducing. A tofu can produce a tofu child several times in their life. Tofus cannot produce children once a tofu younger than them has started reproducing. We define a descendant of a tofu to be either itself, any of its children, any of its children's children, and so on.

A tofu named Batman has descendants. We label each descendant with integers from to . Note that the labels do not correspond in any way to the ages of the tofus. For each descendant, you will be given the list of its children. The children in each list will be listed in order from oldest to youngest.

From the data and facts above, given any two descendants of Batman, it is always possible to deduce which one is older.

Suppose one of the descendants of Batman, say descendant , decided to rename all of his descendants in the following manner. Descendant arranges all her descendants from oldest to youngest (thus she is the first in this list). She decides to rename all her descendants. The first one on the list (i.e., himself) is renamed Jambo #1. The next (if they exist) is renamed Mambo #1. The next is renamed Rambo #1. At this point, he runs out of names so he starts reusing these three names in the same order but with a different number. And so the fourth is renamed Jambo #2. The next few are Mambo #2, Rambo #2, Jambo #3, Mambo #3, and so on.

As clarified earlier, if descendant does not have enough descendants, it will be the case that no one will be called a certain name. Although the possibility that no one gets named Mambo #5 is genuinely disappointing, such an unfortunate incident can definitely occur.
