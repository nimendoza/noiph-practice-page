You and your friends recently got tired of playing Trip to Jerusalem (i.e. Musical Chairs). In order to make things interesting again, you decide to invent a new game. And you call it Reverse Trip to Jerusalem.

Here's how it works. Instead of arranging the chairs in a circle, the friends (including you) arrange yourselves in a circle. Then exactly one person in the circle holds the chair.

For the first round, all of you will be in the circle. The music starts and plays for seconds. Every second, the person holding the chair shall pass it to the person to his left. The person who receives the chair when the music ends is OUT and he/she is not included in the circle in the subsequent rounds. The person to his left then receives the chair. And that's where the chair starts for the second round.

For the round, players remain and the person holding the chair is the person to the left of the most recently outed person. The music starts and plays for seconds. The procedure for passing the chair is the same as the first round. The player who receives the chair when the music ends is OUT.

The person who remains after rounds is the winner.

Of course, you want to win this. It is your dream and you believe that you will survive. The person who initially holds the chair in the first round is Todd. You wish to know where to position yourself in order to win the game.

Input Format
The input consists of two lines. The first line contains a single integer . The second line contains integers, the 's, each separated by single spaces.

Output Format
Output a single line describing the winning position. If the winning position is the position to the left of Todd, then output the integer (Note that ). However, if the winning position is Todd's position, print YOU MUST BE TODD.

Constraints

Note: The time limit is half of the usual time limit.

Sample Input 1 

5
1 2 2 4

Sample Output 1 

2

Sample Input 2 

2
1

Sample Output 2 

YOU MUST BE TODD
